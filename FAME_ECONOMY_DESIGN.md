# FAME & DEMAND REFORM — design doc

*Started 2026-07-12. The economy break-up Daniel called after the commission pass: "stop, and

## 1. Diagnosis (measured at HEAD 18be9de, harness + code)

**Fame is the game's level indicator — and it saturates 70× too early.** Tiers gate licenses,
worker deep-training, customer budgets, and the daily crate; fame IS the progression spine.
But: income = `2/sale × Better Signage × loyalty rep-mults` (compounding — loyalty mults grow
with serves), and **offline paid full rep per sale**, so away time leveled the player. The old
ladder topped at Mythic = 5,000 while the harness measured:

| rung (old min) | median moment (active play) |
|---|---|
| Friendly 25 | 0:00:22 |
| Trusted 75 | 0:02:03 |
| Beloved 200 | 0:07:55 |
| Renowned 500 | 0:16:03 |
| Legendary 1,500 | 0:35:03 |
| Mythic 5,000 | **1:24:37** |

…into a ~96-hour desire curve, with **643k lifetime fame at desire-death — 128× the top rung**.
Daniel's live report matched: fresh start, under an hour, mostly offline gain → Renowned.

**Demand is supply-blind.** The want-pick draws from all *licensed* items with no knowledge of
stockability: ten trade-tier items pull queue demand like gold-tier goods, but their supply is
material-bound. Unsatisfiable demand is generated by construction.

**Scarcity's cost is mispriced.** An out-of-stock wanter burns full patience at the front and
leaves at −1 rep (Bob's auto-wave fires only on *can't-afford*, never out-of-stock). Against
six-figure fame totals, −1 is homeopathic — and the real current cost (the wanter **blocks the
line** while waiting) is an invisible throughput tax the player feels as friction, not system.

**The demand bonus is real but voiceless.** `wantBias: 2` — today's event genuinely doubles its
category's wanters — but since the HUD chip retired it speaks once at dawn (log + bubble) and
never again.

## 2. Goals

1. Fame = the level track: visible, steadily-earned, meaningful across the WHOLE arc, and
   ultimately **spendable at prestige** (the engagement capstone — prestige is a core replay
   hook, not a mop-up sink).
2. Demand tells the truth about supply; scarcity costs something legible; the day's demand has
   an ambient voice.
3. Every pass harness-measured; one system per pass per commit.

## 4. F1a spec (Daniel's Option 3, picked 2026-07-12; offline haircut approved)

**The curve.** `levelThreshold(n) = round(base × growth^(n−1))` lifetime fame to BE level n
(level 0 below the first threshold). Dials: `CONFIG.reputation.levels = { base: 25,
growth: 1.6 }`. Infinite headroom by construction.

**The rungs.** The seven named tiers survive as RUNGS anchored at LEVELS — config rows become
`{ label, level }`; config derives each row's `min` from the curve at load, so every existing
`.min`/index consumer (licenses, budgets, crates, deep training, fame track, sim, suite) keeps
its contract untouched. Placement, calibrated against the harness income model
(rep(t) ≈ 3000·t^1.2 active hours):

| rung | level | min (derived) | design target (active) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Neutral | 0 | 0 | — |
| Friendly | 2 | 40 | first minute |
| Trusted | 6 | 262 | ~10 min |
| Beloved | 10 | 1,718 | ~40 min |
| Renowned | 13 | 7,037 | ~2h |
| Legendary | 17 | 46,117 | ~9–10h |
| Mythic | 20 | 188,895 | ~30h |

**Offline haircut.** `CONFIG.offline.repFraction = 0.5` — offline sales pay half fame ("word of
mouth needs you present"). Gold untouched; fame becomes an active-play-weighted currency.

**Surfaces.** HUD: rung badge gains the level ("Legendary · Lv 17"); the remainder line counts
to the next LEVEL, naming it when the next level is a rung ("to Renowned"). Fame track panel
auto-flows (reads the derived mins).

**Acceptance.** Harness rung moments land near the design targets; levels keep arriving across
the full desire curve; suite green; no gate consumer changes semantics.

## 5. F1b — Prestige spends fame (the engagement capstone)

Daniel's framing, locked: *prestige is one of the goals to keep the game engaging for those who
want to play* — a replay hook, not a graveyard. Design direction (mechanics ship with step 8):

- **The cost is lifetime fame.** Opening a Franchise SPENDS a fame stake (e.g., priced in
  levels: "Franchise at Lv 20+, costs Lv 15's worth"). Fame finally has a sink worthy of its
  income, and the level track resets into a fresh climb that the player CHOSE.
- **What carries: relics** (the standing prestige law — restores are permanent), plus
  franchise-side perks bought with the stake (the Special-of-the-Day repurpose and the
  endgame-audit findings fold in here).
- **What resets:** gold, stock, licenses, workers, materials, the level track — the shop is new;
  the OWNER is famous.
- **Open questions for the step-8 options round:** stake pricing curve; whether monster loyalty
  ledgers (rep mults) carry, halve, or reset; whether expedition capacity is a franchise perk
  (Daniel's note: more expeditions through prestiges).

## 6. F2 — Demand honesty (SHIPPED 2026-07-13, e71beef — Option 1, Option B dials)

The want-pick's item stage gained the SUPPLY weight (`supplyWantWeight`, game.js; dials
`CONFIG.queue.supplyWantBias` — stocked 1 / known 0.7 / unknown 0.4). Acceptance MET and
certified 3× bit-identical: front-blocked out-of-stock time fell from a median 63% of each run
to ~51.5%; trade serve share tracks supply at 14.6–15.0%; serve throughput +13%. THE BIG
MEASURED TRUTH: the first cut (0.4/0.15) flipped market-blind to WEAK (aware −20%) — the old
+48% market edge was mostly the mispriced-scarcity TAX on non-participants, not trade margin.
The Option B floors deliberately keep a partial tax in the non-participant's queue; **+12% is
the market's honest margin at these dials.** Full story: the handoff's 2026-07-13 section.

## 7. F3 — Scarcity teeth (SHIPPED 2026-07-13, 7db7c21 — the re-scoped Option 1)

Shipped as the tier-scaled leave penalty: `leavePenaltyOf` = leavePenalty +
leavePenaltyPerTier × rung index (1 → 7 across the ladder), charged to SPENDABLE fame only —
the lifetime track never falls (suite-pinned through the real leave path), auto-wave and
dismissal penalty-free by law and pin. The fiction-first option measured as REAL teeth: the
drain compounds through perk timing, widening every margin (market-blind +12% → **+21%**,
exp-blind +28% → **+36%**, comm-blind +6.0% → **+12.4%**, 3× bit-identical). The re-scope
history below is retained for the record — the throughput lever (fame-scaled trade shelf
depth; inspection-fullness coupling flagged) and the memory lever (missed-demand decay ledger)
remain documented, unbuilt options if a future certification shows the margin eroding.

The original sketch (fame-scaled leave penalties; a missed-demand counter feeding a
tier-scaled bleed) rested on a premise F2's certification measured to be DIAL-DEPENDENT: after
demand honesty, misses concentrate substantially on the shop that PARTICIPATES (stocked trade
shelves attract full-weight asks and sell out at maxStock 5), while at the first-cut dials the
blind shop's queue was nearly miss-free — a miss-tax there would have INVERTED the gap. At the
shipped 0.7/0.4 floors the blind queue keeps a real stream of unservable floor asks, so the
direction of any miss-cost hinges on where OOS time actually sits blind-vs-aware — now printed
in the market acceptance block (the F3 sizing line). F3's re-scoped brief: close the gap
between the honest +12% and a margin worth the market's ceremony by making PARTICIPATION pay
(or gold-throughput cost), measured before designed. Its options round opens from the sizing
numbers, not from this paragraph. Auto-wave stays penalty-free either way (Bob managing the
line is service, not failure — unchanged law).
